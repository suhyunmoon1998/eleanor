## 3. Non-negotiable source principles

The source material establishes that triggers can arise from at least three channels:

- incoming material, including email, e-service, court, counsel, client, insurer, agency, arbitrator, mediator, medical office, expert, vendor, or other participant;
- outgoing Jack Law work product, including letters, filings, discovery, forms, agreements, and other communications;
- scanned, uploaded, dropped-off, or manually entered hard-copy material.

The operative event is the trigger—not merely “an email arrived” or “a document was scanned.”

Each trigger may produce multiple separate outputs, including:

- rules and calculations;
- several separate calendar entries;
- reminders or ticklers;
- document drafts;
- communications;
- filing or service actions;
- case/list/PHS updates;
- research or review;
- assignments and follow-ups;
- oversight and approval steps;
- completion or close evidence;
- downstream triggers.

Jack Law supplies the hard rules and firm procedures. The application must not silently replace those rules with generic legal research. Legal items that have not been verified must remain clearly labeled “Needs Legal Verification.”

## 9. Interview logic

Store the runtime prompt as a versioned resource file, not a giant inline string.

After every answer, Eleanor should silently:

1. Extract all relevant facts.
2. Atomize independently occurring events.
3. Link them to the chronology, triggers, branches, and downstream work.
4. Apply inheritance and record explicit overrides.
5. Update do-not-repeat memory.
6. Score gaps.
7. Ask the single shortest natural question that closes the highest-value gap.

Gap priority:

A. Missing preceding or following trigger that leaves a chronological gap  
B. Branch or condition changing the deadline, action, or strategy  
C. Deadline, service method, hearing calculation, or replacement date  
D. Filing, service, contact, communication, document, or calendar output  
E. Owner, reviewer, approver, automation boundary, or escalation  
F. Completion evidence and downstream expected event  
G. Exact naming or formatting that can safely wait  

Interview depth modes:

- Express: broad confirmation and only critical follow-up
- Balanced: default
- Teach the Move: invite deeper expert explanation at meaningful judgment points

Efficiency rules:

- one primary question per turn;
- never read the 227-trigger atlas aloud;
- do not ask a fact already answered directly or indirectly;
- do not summarize every answer;
- summarize when closing a family, resolving a contradiction, or moving sections;
- allow “skip,” “park it,” “go back,” “keep going,” “show me the entry,” “what is missing,” “commit that,” and “stop here”;
- preserve long, natural answers and extract several fields from one answer;
- do not force Jack to speak in database terminology.
